## Story block (first person — 470 words)

Hi, I'm Anna, and I have a confession: I've always been the friend who knows where to get it.

You know the one. The person in the group chat who answers "where'd you get that?" before the message is even finished sending. For years, that was just my personality. I'd see a look I loved — a bag, a watch, a pair of shoes that stopped me mid-scroll — and instead of dropping a month's rent on it, I'd go hunting. I'd dig through the internet until I found the version that gave the exact same feeling for a fraction of the price. Then I'd wear it, get the compliments, and quietly feel like I'd cracked a code nobody else knew about.

Here's what I learned doing that for years: looking expensive was never about spending a fortune. It was about taste. It was about knowing which silhouette, which finish, which little detail made a piece feel elevated — and being patient enough to find it. The price tag was never the point. The look was.

So I had this growing notebook in my head (and eventually, my phone) full of finds. The tote that ended my tote search. The watch with quiet old-money energy. The runner that showed up on everyone's FYP three weeks after I'd already been wearing it. My friends kept asking for the links. Then their friends did. At some point it stopped being a hobby and started feeling like a job I'd accidentally been training for my whole life.

That's how Piece of Stass happened.

The name says it all, honestly — stash plus sass. A stash, because I hoard the good stuff so you don't have to dig for it. Sass, because I have opinions about all of it and I'm not shy. Say it out loud and it sounds like "peace of mind," which is exactly the feeling of scoring the look without the markup.

Here's my promise to you. I don't sell everything — I sell the edit. Every single thing in the stash earned its spot because I'd actually wear it, gift it, or talk about it in the group chat. It's curated across everything you wear, carry, and gift, and it's priced so you can say yes without the four-figure regret. Expensive-looking, not expensive. That's the whole point.

I'm not a logo you've heard of. I'm the taste behind the look — confident, a little cheeky, always in the know. Think of me as your friend with the best taste, except now my whole notebook is open to you.

So that's the deal. I found the gems. I'm sharing my stash.

Go raid it.

— Anna
