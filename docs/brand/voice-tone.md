# Piece of Stass — Voice & Tone

> **Status:** Locked v1.0 · Pairs with `brand-guide.md` (read the do/don't language list there first).
> **Voice is constant. Tone flexes by channel.** Voice = our personality (never changes). Tone = the dial we turn up or down depending on where we're speaking.

---

## 1. Voice principles

Our voice is **the friend with the best taste in your group chat.** Confident, a little cheeky, always in the know, never mean, never trying too hard. Five principles:

### 1. Curate, don't catalog
**We are:** an editor with a point of view. We've already picked the winner.
**We are not:** a spec sheet. We don't list features; we tell you why it's the one.
- ✅ "This is the tote that ends the tote search. Buttery, structured, goes with everything."
- ❌ "Faux-leather tote bag. 30cm x 40cm. Available in 4 colors. Magnetic closure."

### 2. Cheeky, never corny
**We are:** witty, wink-wink, self-aware. Humor that lands because it's restrained.
**We are not:** trying-too-hard, meme-stuffed, or cringe. One joke beats five.
- ✅ "Your card is safe. Your willpower is not."
- ❌ "OMG bestie this slay is SO fire no cap fr fr 🔥🔥🔥"

### 3. Confident, not pushy
**We are:** decisive. We state the opinion and let it sit.
**We are not:** a hard-sell infomercial. We never beg, never fake-scarcity-spam.
- ✅ "Stashed before it's trending. You're welcome."
- ❌ "BUY NOW!!! ONLY 2 LEFT!!! DON'T MISS OUT!!! HURRY!!!"

### 4. Accessible, never snobby
**We are:** warm, inclusive, everyone's invited. "Luxury energy" is a feeling we hand out free.
**We are not:** gatekeepy, jargon-y, or elitist. Taste isn't a velvet rope.
- ✅ "Expensive-looking, not expensive. That's the whole point."
- ❌ "For the discerning connoisseur of elevated sartorial sensibilities."

### 5. Honest hype
**We are:** open about what we are — the look for less, the curated stash.
**We are not:** deceptive. We never imply we're a brand we're not (see do/don't list).
- ✅ "Quiet-luxury energy, loud-value price."
- ❌ "Authentic designer quality, indistinguishable from the real thing."

## 2. Tone matrix

Same voice, five different rooms. Columns are the dials we turn.

| Context | Cheeky dial | Energy | Length | Emoji | Job to be done |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Homepage / Hero** | High | High | Ultra-short, punchy | 0–1 | Stop the scroll, set the vibe, one CTA |
| **PDP (product page)** | Medium | Medium-high | Short, scannable, benefit-led | 0–1 | Sell the *one* thing; make the look feel inevitable |
| **Email / SMS** | High | Medium | Personal, one idea per send | 1–2 | Feel like a text from a friend; drive the click |
| **Paid ad / Social** | High | Very high | Hook in 1.5s, vertical-native | 1–3 | Stop thumb, earn the tap, sound-off legible |
| **Customer service** | Low (warm) | Calm | Clear, human, fast | 0–1 | Solve it, no jargon, never blame the customer |

### Tone in practice — same product, five rooms

- **Homepage:** "Raid the stash. New drops daily."
- **PDP:** "The loafer doing the heavy lifting in every clean-girl fit right now. Chunky sole, buttery finish, goes with literally everything. Get the look for less."
- **Email:** "Subject: we found *the* loafer 👀 — The one all over your FYP just hit the stash. Linking before it sells out (it will)."
- **Ad:** "POV: you found the loafer everyone's asking about. The look for less. Tap to raid the stash."
- **CS:** "Totally get it — wrong size is the worst. I've started your free exchange and the new pair ships today. You'll have a tracking link within the hour."

## 4. 20 banned phrases

Never use these — some are legally risky, some are just off-brand cringe. (Trademark rules live in `brand-guide.md`; these are the everyday offenders.)

**Legally risky (never, ever):**
1. "dupe of [brand]" / any "dupe" near a trademark
2. "replica" / "mirror" / "AAA" / "1:1"
3. "knockoff" / "fake" / "counterfeit"
4. "[Brand]-style" / "like [Brand]" / "[Brand]-inspired"
5. "authentic designer" / "genuine [brand] quality"
6. Any named model: "the Birkin," "Air Force," "the Love bracelet"

**Off-brand / cringe (never):**
7. "Cheugy" (using it makes us cheugy)
8. "Slay" / "ate" / "left no crumbs" (overused into death)
9. "Bestie" as a sales tactic
10. "No cap" / "fr fr" / "on god"
11. "Girlies" / "the girls that get it, get it"
12. "Unalive," "seggs," and other algo-speak in owned copy
13. "Affordable luxury" (tired, vague — say "the look for less")
14. "Must-have" / "game-changer" / "obsessed" (filler hype)
15. "Curated *for you*, bestie" (over-personalized fake intimacy)
16. "Hurry, only X left!" fake scarcity (unless literally true)
17. "Click here" (write the actual action)
18. "Synergy," "leverage," "utilize," any corporate-speak
19. "Cheap" (we're *value*, never *cheap*)
20. Excessive ALL CAPS sentences (one word for emphasis, max)

## 5. Emoji policy

Emojis are seasoning, not the meal. They punctuate, they never carry meaning alone (accessibility + screen readers).

### Rules
- **Max per message:** Homepage/PDP 0–1 · Email/SMS 1–2 · Social/Ads up to 3 · CS 0–1.
- **Never** stack the same emoji (🔥🔥🔥). One does the job.
- **Never** replace a word with an emoji in owned UI copy (button labels, nav, errors). Decorative only.
- **Never** rely on emoji for required info ("⚠️" must be paired with text).
- **Place at the end** of a line, not mid-sentence, in marketing copy.

### On-brand emoji set (the short list)
Use these; they fit the cheeky-curated voice:
`👀` (the tea/look) · `🤌` (chef's kiss taste) · `🫶` (love/community) · `✨` (elevated, sparingly) · `🛒` (CTA) · `📩` (email/DM) · `🔖` (the stash/save) · `🧾` (the price flex)

### Off-limits emoji
- `🔥💯😍🥵💅` — exhausted, reads try-hard.
- Skin-tone-specific hand emojis in broad marketing (keep inclusive/neutral).
- Anything that could imply a health/efficacy claim on fragrance or tech.
- No emoji in: legal copy, shipping/returns policy, error states, price displays.

### Quick examples
- ✅ Email subject: "we found *the* loafer 👀"
- ✅ Social caption: "the look for less, dropped daily 🔖"
- ❌ Button: "Add to bag 🛒🔥✨" → just "Add to bag"
- ❌ "Your order shipped 🚀🚀🚀" → "Your order's on the way 📦" (one, optional)
