# Piece of Stass — Brand Guide

> **Status:** Locked v1.0 · Owner: Brand & Creative Director · Domain: pieceofstass.com
> This is the single source of truth for who we are and how we show up. If a decision isn't covered here, default to the personality and the do/don't language list.

---

## 1. The one-liner

**Piece of Stass** is the curated stash of the internet's best lifestyle finds — the look for less, dropped daily, picked by someone with taste.

The name = **stash + sass.** A stash because we hoard the good stuff so you don't have to dig. Sass because we have an opinion and we're not shy about it. Say it out loud and it sounds like "peace of mind" — which is exactly the feeling of scoring the look without the markup.

---

## 2. Mission

To make great taste affordable — curating trend-driven lifestyle finds across everything you wear, carry, and gift, so anyone can look expensive without overthinking it.

## 3. Vision

To become the most-screenshotted shopping destination on the internet for Gen-Z — the first tab you open when you see a look you want and the last word in "where'd you get that."

## 4. Values

1. **Taste is the product.** We don't sell everything. We sell the edit. Curation is the value we add, not a feature.
2. **Access over exclusivity.** Good design shouldn't be gatekept behind a price tag. We democratize the look.
3. **Move at the speed of the feed.** Trends move in days, not seasons. We ship the moment, not the catalog.
4. **Honest hype.** We are confident, never deceptive. We never claim to be a brand we're not. We sell the *vibe*, openly.
5. **Built for the screenshot.** Every product, page, and post is designed to look incredible in a story repost. If it doesn't screenshot well, it isn't done.

Two words, imperative, a little mischievous, ownable, works as a CTA, a hashtag (#RaidTheStash), and a chant. It puts the brand name in the action.

## 8. Do / Don't language list

This is the most important section for legal safety **and** brand consistency. The positioning is *unbranded / inspired-by*. Read this before writing anything.

### ✅ DO say
- "the look for less" / "get the look"
- "inspired by the trend" / "trending right now" / "viral right now"
- "TikTok-famous" / "all over your FYP" / "the one you keep seeing"
- "curated," "the edit," "the stash," "our picks," "hand-picked"
- "designer-energy," "luxury energy," "expensive-looking," "elevated"
- "dupe-free zone" — *only* meaning we sell our own picks (see note below)
- "old-money aesthetic," "quiet-luxury vibe," "clean-girl," "mob-wife energy" (aesthetic *vibes*, not brands)
- "if you love [aesthetic/trend], you'll love this"
- Describe by silhouette, material, color, and vibe: "chunky platform loafer," "buttery faux-leather tote," "iced-out tennis watch"

### ❌ DON'T say (ever — legal + brand)
- **Any trademarked brand name** to describe our product ("like Gucci," "Chanel-style," "Rolex dupe," "Dior-inspired"). Never. Not in copy, alt text, meta tags, SKUs, or ads.
- The words **"dupe," "replica," "fake," "knockoff," "copy," "counterfeit," "mirror,"** or **"AAA"** in reference to a branded item. ("Dupe" culture is tempting but legally radioactive when paired with a brand name — avoid entirely to be safe.)
- "Authentic [Brand]," "genuine," "original" implying it is a named brand's product.
- Any **trademarked logo, monogram, or brand pattern** in imagery, even partially.
- "Designer" as a noun implying a specific house ("designer Gucci"). "Designer-*energy*" as a vibe adjective is fine.
- Model/style names owned by another brand ("the Birkin," "the Speedy," "Air Force," "Jordans," "the Cartier Love").
- Comparative pricing against a named brand ("$3,000 elsewhere, $49 here" *if* it names or pictures the brand).
- Health/efficacy claims on fragrance/tech we can't substantiate.

> **Note on "dupe":** Gen-Z search and talk in dupes, so the temptation is real. Our rule: we win the *aesthetic* search ("clean girl tote," "quiet luxury watch"), never the *brand* search. We never let "dupe" touch a trademark. When in doubt, describe the look, not the label.

### Tone of the language
- **Contractions:** always (we're, you'll, don't).
- **Sentence case** for everything except the wordmark.
- **One exclamation max**, and rarely. Confidence doesn't shout.
- **Oxford comma:** yes.
- **Numbers:** numerals always (better for mobile scanning and prices).

### Our positioning statement
> For Gen-Z and young-millennial shoppers who live in their feed and want the look without the four-figure price tag, **Piece of Stass** is the curated, influencer-picked stash of trend-driven lifestyle finds across everything you wear, carry, and gift. Unlike apparel-only fast-fashion (Princess Polly, Edikted), occasion-bound retailers (Lulus), or aspirational-priced multi-brand stores (Revolve), we deliver one taste-led edit across **8 categories** at prices built for impulse — the luxury *energy*, never the markup.

### The whitespace we own
- **Multi-category lifestyle curation** (footwear, watches, bags, men's, women's, kids', fragrance, tech) under one taste-led voice — competitors are apparel-mono-category.
- **Aesthetic-agnostic** — we ride *every* micro-trend (quiet luxury, mob wife, clean girl, blokecore), not one fixed Y2K look.
- **"Look for less" without dupe language** — we capture dupe-culture demand through aesthetic search while staying trademark-clean.
- **Founder-as-curator** (Anna L.) — a real point of view, not a faceless catalog.
